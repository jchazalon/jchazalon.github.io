Short biography
I received an engineer degree (2008), then a Ph.D. degree (2013), both in Computer Science, from INSA Rennes. Then I worked as a research engineer at the University of La Rochelle, France, from 2013 to 2018. Since 2018, I'm a lecturer at EPITA, with both teaching and research activities. My research interests are in the field of document image analysis, with a focus on historical documents and interactive information extraction. I am also interested in the evaluation of algorithms and systems, and in the promotion of open and reproducible research practices. I am involved in the organization of scientific events, such as ICDAR competitions and occasional workshops. I enjoy working with many talented students and colleagues, from various fields, countries and cultures, and I am always open to new collaborations.
